As Remittances Increase, Central American Economies Suffer

NINAR TAHA

November 24, 2025

Remittances to Central America have significantly increased since the pandemic due to limited employment opportunities. For Guatemala, Honduras, and El Salvador, this pattern has only contributed to a growing reliance on remittances for each country’s overall economy.

Immanuel Ness, a political economist at Brooklyn College, says that the increase to the percentage of remittances of each country’s GDP is not a positive thing. “It shows that the economy is unable to develop on its own, and it’s dependent on other countries for its development,” says Ness.

He says that countries do not tend to develop as a consequence of migration, and there is no evidence to show that they are building up industries, technologies or agriculture. He says it’s still too soon to understand the impact of the current immigration crackdown on remittances, but Ness expects that people will still migrate wherever there are job opportunities, regardless of the administration.
